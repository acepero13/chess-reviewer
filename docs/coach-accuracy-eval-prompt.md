# Coach Accuracy Evaluation — LLM Prompt

Use this document when you want to verify that the app's coaching tips are accurate and pedagogically sound.

---

## How to use

1. Copy the **Prompt** section below.
2. Replace every `<placeholder>` with the real values from the app (see **What to provide**).
3. Paste the filled prompt into your LLM of choice (Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, etc.).

---

## Prompt

```
You are a chess coach evaluator. I will give you a position and a coaching comment
generated by an automated chess review app. Your job is to judge whether the coaching
comment is accurate, relevant, and pedagogically sound for the given position.

---

## Position

FEN: <FEN string of the position BEFORE the move was played>

Move played: <move in SAN or UCI notation, e.g. "Nf3" or "g1f3">
Color to move: <White | Black>
Move number: <e.g. "14" (White's 14th move)>

Game phase: <Opening | Middlegame | Endgame>

Engine evaluation BEFORE move: <e.g. "+0.45" (centipawns / 100), or "N/A" if unavailable>
Engine evaluation AFTER move:  <e.g. "-1.20", or "N/A" if unavailable>
Centipawn loss: <e.g. "165 cp loss" or "N/A">

Engine best move (if known): <e.g. "Rxe4" or "N/A — do not reveal if you want an unbiased check>

---

## Coaching trigger type

<One of: SAFETY | CANDIDATE_MOVES | WORST_PIECE | FORCING_MOVE | OPPONENT_PLAN |
PRE_MOVE_CHECKLIST | ROOK_ACTIVATION | IMPULSE_CONTROL | CANDIDATE_SEARCH | CCT_CHECK |
MISSED_TACTIC | HANGING_PIECE | KING_SAFETY | ENDGAME_PRINCIPLE | OPENING_DEVIATION |
STRATEGIC_MISTAKE | TIME_PRESSURE | MISSED_WIN>

---

## App's coaching output

**Title:** <title shown in the app, e.g. "King Safety Check">

**Description:** <description paragraph shown in the app>

**Coaching questions shown to the user:**
1. <question 1>
2. <question 2>
3. <question 3>

**Conceptual hint (shown on request):** <hint text>

---

## What I want you to evaluate

Answer each of the following five questions with a short paragraph (3–5 sentences max).
End with an overall verdict: ✅ Accurate | ⚠️ Partially accurate | ❌ Inaccurate.

1. **Trigger accuracy** — Is the coaching trigger type correct for this position?
   Given the position and the centipawn swing, was this the right *type* of moment to flag?

2. **Description accuracy** — Is the description factually correct for this specific position?
   Does it describe what is actually happening on the board, or is it generic/misleading?

3. **Question relevance** — Are the three questions useful guides toward the actual best move?
   Would answering them lead a player to find (or understand) the correct idea?

4. **Hint quality** — Is the conceptual hint accurate and does it avoid giving away the engine line?
   Is it the right level of abstraction (concept, not move)?

5. **Overall verdict** — Rate the coaching output as a whole.
   Would this comment genuinely help an improving player understand what went wrong?
```

---

## What to provide and format

| Field | Where to find it | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **FEN** | Debug view or `PositionAnnotation.fen` in the DB | Standard FEN string |
| **Move played** | Move list | SAN (e.g. `Nf3`) preferred; UCI (e.g. `g1f3`) also works |
| **Color / move number** | Move list | Plain text |
| **Game phase** | Inferred from move number or `OpeningClassifier` output | Opening / Middlegame / Endgame |
| **Engine evals** | `TruthMapEntry.evalCp` before and after the move | Decimal, e.g. `+0.45` or `-1.20` |
| **Centipawn loss** | Difference of the two evals × 100 | Integer, e.g. `165 cp loss` |
| **Engine best move** | `TruthMapEntry.bestMove` | SAN or UCI — omit if you want an unbiased check |
| **Trigger type** | `CoachingTrigger.typeName()` or `CriticalMoment.ReasonCategory` | One of the enum values listed in the prompt |
| **Title / Description / Questions / Hint** | The coaching panel shown on screen | Copy-paste the exact text |

### Minimal example (fill-in)

```
FEN: r1bqk2r/ppp2ppp/2np1n2/2b1p3/2B1P3/2NP1N2/PPP2PPP/R1BQK2R w KQkq - 0 7
Move played: Bg5
Color to move: White
Move number: 7

Game phase: Opening

Engine evaluation BEFORE move: +0.20
Engine evaluation AFTER move:  -0.85
Centipawn loss: 105 cp loss

Engine best move: N/A

Coaching trigger type: OPPONENT_PLAN

Title: Opponent's Intent
Description: Your opponent just improved their position. Stop and understand why.
Coaching questions:
1. What is the strategic idea behind that last move?
2. Does it create a new threat you must deal with?
3. What plans does it open or close for both sides?
Conceptual hint: Look at what squares your opponent's last move controls or attacks.
  Does it threaten to win material or restrict one of your pieces?
```

---

## Notes

- You can omit the engine best move if you want the LLM to evaluate the hint quality
  without already knowing the answer.
- For **Guided Discovery** positions (where the app freezes navigation and asks the user
  to find the move), always include the engine eval so the LLM can judge whether the
  questions actually lead toward it.
- For **proactive coaching triggers** (SAFETY, CCT_CHECK, CANDIDATE_MOVES, etc.) the
  centipawn loss may be zero — that is expected; these fire on *pattern recognition*,
  not on mistakes.
